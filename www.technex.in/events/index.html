D Aeroglisseur
Introduction

Design and fabrication of a remote-controlled, wireless hovercraft or an ACV (Air-Cushion Vehicle) which can cruise, race, move on any track and compete. The vehicle will be tested for top-end speed, good acceleration, manoeuvrability and handling & control on a path consisting of challenging terrains like plain & rough surface, water, slope ups & slope downs, obstacles, speed breakers (bumps) & gravels.
The event consists of two rounds:
1. Qualifier Round
2. Finale (Racing Round)

Rules

Only one member will control the hover during the entire run from each participating team.
The dimensions of the hovercraft should be strictly taken into consideration and teams exceeding the given dimensions will be penalised/disqualified.
If during the run, hovercraft is jammed or trapped with any part of arena, one member of controlling team must put the hovercraft back on the last check point crossed but only after the permission of the organizers. During the process, time will continue and the time lost won’t be adjusted.
Only charging points will be provided by the organising team. Participants should carry their own charging equipment.
To prevent interference between the transmitters during the race, participants must use remote with frequency of band spectrum 2.4GHz.
Participants are strongly recommended to bring spare parts to repair damages or replace faulty parts.
Teams should have extra batteries for backup. No extra time will be given for charging the battery.
No parts of the hovercraft will be provided/arranged by the organising team in case of any damage.
Decision taken by the organising team will be final and cannot be questioned.
Any misbehaviour or violence will directly lead to disqualification from the event and all other activities of TECHNEX.
Rules are subjected to change; hence participants are advised to update themselves regularly by visiting the website. Any change will be highlighted clearly.

Introduction

"Rube Goldberg, a comically involved, complicated invention, laboriously contrived to perform a simple operation"

-Webster's new world dictionary

“Upside Down”, put the world in disorder! Tired of everything being so simple? So, let your crazy mind rule you, turn the world upside down. Put together all the lunatic stuff and justify its worth by forging it to do the simplest tasks.
To all the fanatics out there, get a chance to exhibit how you perceive the world by unleashing yourselves in The Goldberg’s Alley Challenge at Technex ’19. We accept the craziest of ideas and yes, we do reward them.
Got you hooked yet?

Task

To design a Goldberg’s machine capable of completing as many tasks as possible from the given set of three tasks.
Three Aims:-
Shooting a dart onto the dart board situated 1.5m away from the arena, using a dart gun.
Knocking out bowling pins situated 2m away from the arena.
Hitting a hammer on the hit hammer game machine.

Rules

Dart board will be fixed on a table 1.5 m away from the arena, height of its center being as per participant’s requirement, ranging from 30 cm to 80 cm above ground. The points will be awarded according to the region where the dart hits the dart board.
Bowling pins will be placed in the conventional manner, 2m away from the arena. The ball for hitting the pins will be provided by us only. The points will be awarded according to the number of bowling pins knocked out.

Hydracs
Introduction

Not all mechanism in this world require motors,circuits,components etc to perform a task. Some of these problems can be resolved using the power of hydraulics as well. For an instance, we often see cranes and bulldozers being used for lifting heavy loads. Then why not induce the concept of liquid engineering in its working! Therefore Technex'19 brings before you the event Hydracs where you get a platform to implement your hydraulic knowledge. Use some wooden scraps,syringes and your creativity to build your own JCB that uses only hydraulic energy. Move your bot all around the arena and lift some loads under a particular scenario to compete in various rounds!

Task

i. There will be 5 different rounds of increasing difficulty.
ii. The first round is qualifiers round. Top 32 teams will be selected on point’s basis for the next round.
iii. The second round is the round is of 32. Top 16 teams will be selected on points basis for the next round.
iv. The Third round is the round of 16. Top 8 teams will be selected for semi-finals on the basis of number of points earned by the teams.
v. In the semi-finals round teams will be judged on the basis of number of points earned and top 4 teams will be selected for the finals.
vi. The final round will be a point-based round. Winners will be decided on the basis of maximum number of points earned in the round.

Rules

 I. Force responsible for the entire task done by the bot should be produced only bymeans of hydraulic pressure.
II. Bot’s body may be made by using wood, hardboard, aluminium, pipes, and syringes, or any other relevant material.
III. There are no height and weight restrictions for the bot.
IV. Any means of energy other than the hydraulic energy is strictly prohibited.
V. Use of motors, battery, spring, string or rubber will lead to disqualification of the bot.
VI. Team size is restricted to a maximum of four members and minimum of one member.
VII. The teams will be given a maximum of two minutes for setting up their bots before starting off the round.
VIII. All dimensions shown in the arenas are in cm.
IX. In any dispute the decision of the coordinators of the event will be the final decision.

Introduction

“A margin of life is developed by Nature for all living things- including man. All life forms obey Nature’s demands- except man, who has found ways of ignoring them.”
- Eugene M. Poirot

On these lines, PAHAL has come up with an event Adhyayan for Technex’19.It aims to bring together the young minds in a bid to solve the problems faced by India due to land contamination and to come up with a solution that can be applied for the betterment of those who are affected by those issues.

Contaminated land means land that has a hazardous substance in or on it that
1. Has significant adverse effects on the environment; or
2. Is reasonably likely to have significant adverse effects on the environment.

People, animals and the environment can be exposed to hazardous substances on contaminated land by direct contact with contaminated soil, swallowing food or water from contaminated environment, etc. Thus causing limited use of land, corrosion that may be threatening to buildings or reduce land value, no adequate remedy is available to curb these social evils. Therefore Adhyayan invites you to get rid of this problem and provide an impactful solution that will help the community you live in.
